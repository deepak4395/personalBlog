## The Shloka

**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
अयनेषु च सर्वेषु यथाभागमवस्थिताः ।
भीष्ममेवाभिरक्षन्तु भवन्तः सर्व एव हि ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
ayaneṣu ca sarveṣu yathābhāgamavasthitāḥ |
bhīṣmam evābhirakṣantu bhavantaḥ sarva eva hi ||

**Translation:**
Now, all of you, remaining in your respective positions in the various divisions of the army, must give full support to Grandfather Bhishma.

## Explanation

### Context
This verse (1.11) appears in the first chapter of the Bhagavad Gita, titled "Arjuna Vishada Yoga," or “The Yoga of Arjuna’s Despair.” Sanjaya is narrating the scene of the Kurukshetra battlefield to King Dhritarashtra. Duryodhana, observing the Pandava army's formation, expresses his concerns to Dronacharya about their strength. He then attempts to bolster the morale of his own forces by highlighting Bhishma's leadership and urging everyone to support him strategically. This verse directly follows Duryodhana's assessment of the opposing army and serves as a call to action for his own troops.

### Deeper Understanding
Duryodhana is trying to rally his troops by emphasizing the importance of their strategic positioning and the necessity of protecting Bhishma, the commander-in-chief. While seemingly a simple military instruction, the verse highlights several deeper layers:

*   **Leadership and Responsibility:** Duryodhana acknowledges Bhishma's crucial role. This underscores the importance of strong leadership in any endeavor. However, it also subtly reveals Duryodhana's insecurity. He feels the need to constantly reassure himself and his troops about their strength.
*   **Unity and Cohesion:** The verse emphasizes the need for all soldiers to support Bhishma. This highlights the importance of unity and cohesion within a group to achieve a common goal. Without collective effort and mutual support, even the strongest leader may falter.
*   **Strategic Thinking:** The call to remain in respective positions signifies the importance of strategic thinking. Each individual has a role to play, and by fulfilling that role effectively, they contribute to the overall success of the operation. This signifies the need for planning and understanding one's responsibilities.

This verse, although spoken in the context of war, holds lessons applicable to various aspects of life. It suggests that success depends not only on individual strength but also on strategic planning, unified action, and strong leadership.

### Practical Application
1.  **Teamwork in the Workplace:** In a team project, ensure everyone understands their role and responsibilities. Support the team leader and work collaboratively to achieve the project goals. Just as Duryodhana urged his soldiers to protect Bhishma, support your team leader's decisions and contribute your best to the collective effort.
2.  **Family Dynamics:** In a family, each member has a role to play in maintaining harmony and achieving common goals. Support the head of the family (or the person taking on a leadership role) and work together to address challenges and celebrate successes. For instance, during a family crisis, each member should support the one leading the effort to resolve the situation.
3.  **Community Involvement:** When participating in community initiatives, understand your role and contribute your skills and resources to support the overall objective. Support the leaders of the initiative and work collaboratively with other members to achieve the desired outcome. If volunteering for a local charity, take your assigned role seriously and support the team lead in their efforts.
